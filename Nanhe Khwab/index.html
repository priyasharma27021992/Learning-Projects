Our Mission

Literacy is a key for socio-economic progress, and the Indian literacy rate has grown to 79.31% (2011 census). Despite government programmes, India's literacy rate increased only "sluggishly". The 2011 census, indicated a 2001–2011 decadal literacy growth of 9.2%, which is slower than the growth seen during the previous decade. An old analytical 1990 study estimated that it would take until 2060 for India to achieve universal literacy at then-current rate of progress. Do you really want to wait these many years? Also, what we feel is all the problems of a Country like Poverty, Criminalism, Racism etc can be solved if the citizens of the Country are well Educated. Our aim is to grow children into productive citizens that use their knowledge, talents, and learned skills to sustain themselves and help others while pushing the human race forward in areas of equality, equity, and harmony.

Discover

Nanhe Khwab Charitable Trust is a registered trust under Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950. Nanhe Khwab is a volunteer driven not-for-profit organization promoting development in field of Child Education for the underprivileged. We currently work from Delhi/NCR/Gurgaon, Pune and Mumbai.

We are associated with a School in Gurgaon called Pragati(under an NGO called AID) for underprivileged kids which provides the basic kindergarten Education to the kids between the age of 5-12 years. And when the kid is ready to start with the formal Education, we get the kid admitted to a formal school. There is a whole process of selecting a School, getting the Sponsor and making sure that the kid has everything he/she needs to study. We organize mentoring sessions for them, arrange for tutions, extra curricular activities, books etc.

How A Lift Encounter Changed A Kid's Life

January 12, 2019 By Priya Sharma

I still remember the day when I met a kid in the lift of my locality, he was on his way to deliver some groceries to a flat. Sharing the conversation I had with him that day. Me: What's your name? Kid: My name is Vikas didi. ... Me: Why do you work in the shop? Why don't you study? What do your parents do? Till what grade have you studied? Vikas: My father is no more didi and my mother works as a maid so she cannot afford my fees that is why I work at the shop. I have studied till 6th and I would like to study more if given a chance. Me: Will you study if I teach you? Vikas(Happily): Of course didi. The next thing I remember is teaching him for 4-5 months after his shift at the shop, convincing his mother to let him study, getting him admitted to 7th class and sponsoring his whole Education through my amazing circle of friends. Fast forward, after 7 years, he has passed 12th Class but due to some issues he could not get admission in College. Although he is a smart kid and got a really nice paying job at Elite Aircon.

When Rishu got some of his vision back

December 10, 2019 By Priya Sharma

Rishu is 5 years old and couldn't see clearly and practically had zero vision before we could not got his operation day. ... We got to know about Rishu from AID's another School where he was studying. Their was an issue in his eyes which was congenital and his parents have the same problem but we wanted Rishu to be able to see so that he is able to see this amazing world and study well( he stopped coming to School as his vision became really poor with the time). We got his check-up done in Shroff Eye Centre in East Kailash. The operation was pretty expensive and costing 96000 but with the help of family and friends we got his one eye operated and his half vision is restored. We are soon planning to get his second operation done as well.
